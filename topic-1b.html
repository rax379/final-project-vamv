Practicing in reading food labels will be a good habit in making smart choices in the supermarket and in the long run to prolong your life. Food labels are contained with information that describes the contents of the product, but understanding how to read them is essential to avoid wrong choices. In this guide, you will learn how each section of the food label is the make up of ingredients and nutrients and how it affects your diet. Remember according to the FDA and USDA, the “average person’s diet” consists of 2,000 calories. Are you reading your food labels correctly?
